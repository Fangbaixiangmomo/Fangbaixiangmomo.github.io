About

I'm Haimo Fang (方海漠), an Economics undergraduate at Fudan University interested in statistical machine learning and econometrics. My current work studies statistical inference and uncertainty quantification for ensemble methods, including gradient boosting and explainable boosting machines. Lately, I've also been exploring Bayesian viewpoints for Transformers.

Research

  • Statistical Inference for Gradient Boosting Regression — Haimo Fang, Kevin Tan, Giles Hooker; NeurIPS 2025 (Poster).
    A frequentist inference framework for gradient boosting: Boulevard-style regularization with dropout/parallel training yields CLTs, enabling built-in confidence & prediction intervals and fast variable-importance tests (with scalable kernel sketching).
  • Statistical Inference for Explainable Boosting Machines — Haimo Fang, Kevin Tan, Jonathan Pipping, Giles Hooker; AISTATS 2026 (Poster).
    Inference for EBMs: Boulevard-regularized boosting converges to feature-wise kernel ridge regression with asymptotic normality, giving confidence intervals for each learned univariate effect with computation in histogram/bin space.
